Edgar Schmued

Designer of Modern Aircraft

Edgar Schmued was instrumental to the development of mid-20th century military aircraft. From propeller-based airplanes to jet fighters, Schmued designed several war planes used in World War II and the Korean War. These designs were used in many theatres of war and remain popular in U.S. military and aviation circles.

Here is a timeline of Edgar Schmued's life:

1899 - Born in Hornbach, Germany.

1909 - First sees a flying airplane. Decides on pursuing a career in aviation.

1925 - Travels to Brazil and finds work at General Aviation of the General Motors Corporation.

1931 - Moves to the United States through a sponsor. Begins work in the New Jersey-based Fokker Aircraft Corporation of America (also owned by General Motors). This company would later be sold and formed into North American Aviation (NAA).

1935 - While traveling together with his wife, Luisa, by car to NAA's new Los Angeles headquarters, the couple get into a severe automobile accident. Schmued's wife is killed, and he himself is seriously injured.

1936 - Begins work at NAA with the company's president, James Howard "Dutch" Kindelberger. Schmued is employed as a preliminary aircraft designer

1940 - Designs and develops the NA-73X, a prototype military aircraft that would eventually become the United State's Army Air Forces' P-51 Mustang fighter. The Mustang would operate in several theatres of World War II.

1944 - 1945 - Assists in the development of the XP-86 jet fighter prototype. This would later become the US Air Force's F-86 Sabre. The Sabre was later used extensively during the Korean War.

1952 - Leaves NAA to work for Northrop Corporation as Vice President of Engineering.

1957 - Retires from Northrop Corporation. Before this he contributed to developing the US Navy's F-5A/B Freedom Fighter jet design. This design had an extremely long service life, in use worldwide for over 60 years.

1985 - Shortly after retiring from years of continuing independent consulting work for both the US government and private companies, Schmued dies at the age of 85 in Oceanside, California.

1991 - On September 14th, 1991, Schmued is inducted into the International Aerospace Hall of Fame.


"Well, Dutch, don't let us build an obsolete airplane, let's build a new one. We can design and build a better one." - Edgar Schmued
